Alis

The Nation of Alis is the largest nation in Torinth, both in size and population. It is known as the birthplace of many of the world's technological innovations.

History

Alis has been known as the technological hub of Torinth for all of history. Major innovations, such as the airship, the arcane battery, the comm, and more can all be traced to Alis. While others, like the Vespans, made similar innovations, Alis inventions were easily mass produced, making them available to the world at large. As the companies making these devices grew richer and more powerful, they began to make company towns, which in turn let them successfully petition for political power. Alis became the center of commerce, and populations boomed. People from all over came to live there, to experience the magical and technological wonders of Alis.

Government

Alisport is governed by the Alis Senate, which is formed by a combination of elected officials and representatives from major corporations and other notable organizations, such as the Alis Academy for the Advancement of the Arcane Arts. They meet in Alisport and vote on issues of government.

Alis Locations

Alisport

The capital city of Alis. Alisport is a immense towering city made in the valley of coastal mountains. The city exists in multiple layers, each containing many stories of toweing buldings. The layers are separated by floating islands, towed here to act as new foundations when the buildings couldn't get any taller.

History

Alisport is the largest, and one of the oldest, cities in the world. Legend says that the city was once the seat of power for the god of craft and innovation, when the gods still resided on the Material Plane. Historically the population was mainly dwarves and gnomes, but Alisport has become the most diverse city in the world.

History

Port Sintague was once on the path of a major trade route. It drew in large amounts of traffic and industry as a combination of location and the large amounts of valuable minerals found there. However, once the mines dried up, most of the industry pulled out, and much of the ship traffic was lost as they diverted to more lucrative locations. To try and attract ships back to the area, laws were laxed, and a blind eye was turned to most crime, so long as they payed their fees and didn't cause trouble for civilians. This revitalized Port Sintague as the hive of scum and villany it is today.
